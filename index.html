Pollution in Delhi, Reuters Graphics

The photos in this project are from a camera that is located on the Parsvnath Capital Towers building in Delhi, India. The air pollution data in this project was recorded by the nearby Central Pollution Control Board air quality monitoring station, which measured air particulate matter as a method of quantifying air pollution.

There are several visual elements used in this piece. First, the article is accompanied by a series of photos that give the reader an idea of the amount of smog in Delhi on any given day. The article also has a histogram showing air particulate matter that can be related to a picture, which allows the reader to draw their own conclusions and see for themselves the extremity of the city's pollution problems. Lastly, the article has a range plot chart which displays the level of pollution in Delhi compared to other Indian locations, which gives the reader context for the issue that they might not already have. Overall, this piece does a fantastic job of showing and not telling.

The key message of this dataviz project is to convey a commonly discussed topic in a new medium. This piece is effective because it uses unique storytelling methods, like relating photographs to air pollution level charts, which is intriguing and educational.

This piece was visceral because it gave a visual aid to an issue that I usually see described in words. Such rampant pollution is easy to overlook when you don't have a good visual idea of what that looks like in a city of millions of people. The accompanying data let the reader leisurely scroll through and explore the findings at their own pace, drawing their own conclusions. By the end of this piece, I was even more inquisitive about a topic that previously rarely crossed my mind, and I believe that is a sign of a good article.
